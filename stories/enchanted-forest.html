Maya's ghostly form solidifies with your words, as if your belief in her right to choose gives her strength. "You're right. I've been so focused on what I'm supposed to do that I never asked what I actually want to do."

Together, you turn away from the mystical grove and walk back toward town. The forest tries to hold you—paths shift, voices call out warnings, and the very air seems to thicken with supernatural resistance. But your determination to reject the predetermined path proves stronger than the ancient magic trying to enforce it.

When you emerge from the grove, Maya is fully solid again, fully human. The town doesn't fall into chaos, the world doesn't end—nothing catastrophic happens at all. Sometimes, it turns out, the scariest choice is simply saying "no" to something that claims to be inevitable.

Years later, Maya becomes a researcher into supernatural phenomena, helping others who face similar "destiny" situations find alternatives to sacrifice and submission. The Whispering Grove grows quiet and dormant, its power fading without someone to feed it with fear and acceptance. Maybe some burdens are only as real as we allow them to be.

The supernatural threat is contained in a place where it can't affect innocent people, and the guardian family can finally break the cycle of sacrifice.
